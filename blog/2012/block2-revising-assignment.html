Revising Assignment

2012-10-29 by James Adney

Part 1: Rough Draft

First, create a rough draft of your paper (approximately two pages long). Don't try to make this draft perfect. Don't worry about spelling, grammar, just focus on getting your words onto paper.

Part 2: Begin Revising

Take a break after writing your rough draft. Walk away from it so you can come back later with a fresh perspective.

After your break (later that day or, preferably, another day) read your rough draft again and answer these questions:

  1. Is the purpose of your paper clear from your draft?

  2. Is your paper organized well? Does it follow a clear pattern?

  3. What should you focus on more in your final paper than you did in your draft?

  4. What should you focus on less in your final paper than you did in your draft?

Final Notes

  • I would prefer that you write out your rough draft by hand, but if you prefer you may type it on the computer and print it. Make sure that if you type it on the computer it is double-spaced.

  • You may answer your questions on a sheet of paper, or on the computer, and turn it in with your rough draft. If you prefer, you may print out this worksheet and answer the questions on it instead.

  • If you have any questions about the assignment, feel free to use the comment area below to ask me or other students for clarification.
